About Crosscut Festival

PACCAR Atrium in the Pigott Building

As Puget Sound’s population grows at breakneck speed, frank discussion is more important than ever. The Crosscut Festival brings together some of the boldest names in politics, business and social justice activism, interviewed live on stage by some of the finest journalists in the Pacific Northwest. Organized by the news outlet Crosscut and hosted by Seattle University, the festival will play to a diverse mix of civic leaders and college students from across the state.

Pigott Building

The Crosscut Festival is a one-evening and one-day event – February 2nd and 3rd – that will bring together some of the best minds and biggest names in the Northwest and around the nation to tackle some of the most pressing issues of our times. We will be shining a spotlight on solutions by staging vital, provocative conversations. We expect 1,500 people to attend, including hundreds of college students. The list of special guest speakers will feature governors, mayors and other elected officials, as well as business leaders and cultural luminaries.

The speakers at the 2018 Crosscut Festival share a history of leadership, reform, innovation and public service. When they take the stage at Seattle University on February 2nd they will be on camera, live on social media and at the center of the national and regional conversations on the pressing issues of our time. They will be in front of hundreds of interested students and other citizens, and the keynote track will be recorded and broadcast to the world online and via social media.
